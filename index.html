Types of personal insurance policies


Insurance is a means of protection from financial loss. Insurance policies, in turn, are agreements between the insurer and the insured to provide a specific type of coverage for a specified period. Insurance can be used to hedge against many types of risks including business interruption due to fire or natural disaster, accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D), automobile liability, homeowners insurance, life insurance, and more. Insurance policies typically fall into one of two categories: indemnity (aka “no-fault”) or cost reimbursement (aka “managed care”). Indemnity policies reimburse you for expenses after an accident – such as your car repairs following an auto collision – while managed care plans require you to pay out-of-pocket expenses first and then seek reimbursement from the insurance company.

Insurance is a necessary evil for most people.
